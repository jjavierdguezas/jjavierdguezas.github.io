About Me

My name is José Javier Rodríguez Zas. I'm years old. I majored in Computer Science at the University of Havana on july 2017. I was an Intern there, teaching Introduction to Programming to newbie students of the Computer Science degree. I have also done some research on fields like Computer Graphics, Computer Animation and Procedural Content Generation. I have been recognized in some events as the Student Scientific Fair (2016) and the XXVI National Scientific Computer Competition (2016). Likewise, I participated in the ACM-ICPC programming contests reaching 12th place (out of a total of 46 teams) in the The 2014 ACM-ICPC Caribbean Finals.

I'm also an active web developer. I am a Windows user but I work with Linux very well. I can code in C#, Java, Python, Ruby and other popular programming languages. Also, I can use the most common web development frameworks as ASP.NET MVC, Rails and Django. I prefer to work in backend but I know enough to work in the frontend too. I've recently fallen in love with .NetCore, ReactJS and TypeScript, I think they work very well together.

I like to learn new things and I like to think that I learn quickly. However, must of the time I'm just coding, either on some project, or a new idea, or just for fun.
